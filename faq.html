Beach Wedding FAQ

Your Questions, Answered

Do we need a beach permit for our ceremony?

Many Florida Gulf Coast beaches require a permit for ceremonies, arches, or chair setups. Requirements vary by city and park. We’ll confirm the rules for your chosen location and guide you through any steps.

What’s the best time of day for a beach wedding?

Golden hour—about 1–2 hours before sunset—offers flattering light, cooler temperatures, and dreamy photos. Morning is quieter and cooler; midday is brightest and warmest.

What if it rains or there’s inclement weather?

We plan weather backups such as a covered pavilion or nearby indoor option, and monitor forecasts to adjust timing when possible. We’ll review the plan with you in advance.

How many guests can we have?

It depends on the specific beach, parking, and local guidelines. Elopements fit almost anywhere; for larger groups we’ll recommend beaches with more space and amenities.

Can you help with photography, flowers, and décor?

Yes—our packages include essentials and optional upgrades like bouquets, arches, tiki torches, and extended photography. We’ll tailor styling to your beach and vision.

Is the beach accessible for guests with limited mobility?

Many locations feature ramps, boardwalks, nearby parking, or beach wheelchairs. Tell us your needs and we’ll suggest the most accessible spots and timing.

How do Florida marriage licenses work?

Florida licenses are valid statewide for 60 days. Non-residents typically have no waiting period; Florida residents may have a short wait without a premarital course. We’ll provide links and file your license after the ceremony.

Can we customize a package?

Absolutely. Mix-and-match photo coverage, arches, seating, unity ceremonies, and more. Share your wish list and we’ll build a plan that fits your style and budget.

Which beaches do you serve?

We frequently serve Clearwater Beach, Sand Key Park, St. Pete Beach, Pass-a-Grille, Fort De Soto, Indian Rocks, Madeira Beach, Redington, Siesta Key, Lido Beach, Anna Maria Island, and more.

How far in advance should we reserve our date?

Peak months (March–May, October–November) book quickly. Reserve 4–6+ months ahead for elopements and 6–12 months ahead for weekend sunset ceremonies that need permits or seating.

Are alcohol or glass allowed on public beaches?

Most public beaches restrict alcohol and prohibit glass. Plan any champagne toasts at a nearby restaurant or private venue. We’ll advise based on your exact beach rules.

Can we use a drone for photos or video?

Drone rules vary by jurisdiction and site type. Some parks restrict launches/landings or require written permission. Tell us early and we’ll confirm what’s allowed for your ceremony spot.

What should we know about sea turtle nesting season?

During nesting season, beaches follow wildlife-safe practices: minimize lighting, avoid obstacles on the sand, and remove décor promptly. We’ll suggest turtle-friendly styling and timing.

Can we release balloons, sky lanterns, or confetti?

Outdoor balloon releases, sky lanterns, and confetti are generally not allowed on public beaches. Choose eco-friendly alternatives like bubbles, ribbon wands, or real flower petals.

Are fires, tiki torches, or candles allowed?

Open flames are restricted and often require permission as part of an approved setup. We’ll confirm what’s permitted and offer wind-safe lighting alternatives.

Can we bring our dog to the ceremony?

Most swimming beaches do not allow dogs; designated dog beaches do, with leash rules. If your pup is part of the day, we’ll recommend the right access points.

Where do guests park, and are there fees?

Major beaches have public lots; many charge hourly or daily fees. We’ll send parking details and recommend earlier arrival for sunset ceremonies.

Can we play music or use a microphone?

Small battery speakers and a handheld mic are usually fine at conversational levels. Local noise ordinances apply—our team sizes sound to fit the beach and rules.

How long is the ceremony and photo time?

Most ceremonies run 10–20 minutes. Plan 30–60 minutes for portraits, ideally around golden hour for the best light.

What if it’s windy on the day?

We bring wind-smart options and can adjust the exact spot for shelter while staying on the same beach when possible.

What should guests wear and bring?

Breathable fabrics and flat sandals are best. Daytime guests should bring sunscreen and water. Avoid digging large holes and fill any small ones for safety.

Do you perform same-sex weddings, vow renewals, and symbolic ceremonies?

Yes—We tailor the wording and flow for weddings, renewals, and symbolic celebrations.

Can you recommend reception spots after the beach?

Yes. We’ll share nearby restaurants, patios, and private rooms that welcome groups and beach attire.

Can we work with outside photographers or officiants?

We’re flexible and happy to coordinate with your chosen vendors. Some public sites may require vendor insurance—we’ll help with any paperwork.
