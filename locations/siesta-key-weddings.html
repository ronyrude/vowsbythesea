Parking & Timing Tips

  • Large public lots - arrive early on weekends and holidays (mid-afternoon fills fastest).
  • Start 60-90 minutes before sunset for the richest sky color and even light.
  • Build a cushion for Stickney Point Rd traffic during peak season.

Ideal Guest Counts & Seating

  • Great for 2-60 guests. We can add white chairs, simple florals, parasols, or a bamboo arch to many packages.
  • ADA-friendly pathways available at main Siesta Beach access points.

Why Couples Choose a Siesta Key Beach Wedding

Siesta Key is consistently ranked among the best beaches in the United States for its powder-soft quartz sand, shallow turquoise water, and postcard sunsets. Couples love the guest-friendly parking, easy access, and the island's vibrant Village for a fun, walkable reception after a toes-in-the-sand ceremony. Whether you are planning a two-person elopement or a seated gathering with family and friends, we deliver photo-first timelines, simple decor options, and vendor coordination that keeps the day relaxed and on time.

  • Decor made simple: arches, tiki accents, lanterns, parasols, chair sashes, and florals to match your palette.
  • Permits & guidance: we will point you to the right Sarasota County resources and ceremony zones.
  • Photo-first pacing: built-in time for couple portraits, group photos, and a sunset sequence.

Siesta Key Wedding FAQ

Do I need a permit for a beach ceremony?

Most organized ceremonies require a permit and adherence to local beach rules. We will direct you to the correct Sarasota County portal and help you choose compliant setups.

Where is the best spot for photos?

We often pair the main Siesta Beach access for family photos with a short walk toward less-crowded shoreline for couple portraits at golden hour.

What is the rain plan?

We track radar and build buffer time. Common adjustments include shifting to an earlier/later window, choosing a more sheltered access, or a nearby covered meetup if needed.
